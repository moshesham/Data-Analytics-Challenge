# Day 21: The One-Slide Story

## Objective
Master the art of telling a complete, compelling data story in a single, powerful visual designed for an executive audience.

---

## Step-by-Step Guide

### 1. Review the Week's Narrative
- Summarize findings from Days 15â€“20
- Identify the single most important message

### 2. Choose the Core Narrative
- Decide on the story (e.g., "Journals boosts retention by creating a new habit, but does not yet grow overall engagement.")

### 3. Design the Slide
- Title: Assertive, clear takeaway
- Visuals: No more than 3 key charts/numbers
- Keep it simple and focused

### 4. Write Speaker Notes
- Script for explaining the slide in under 60 seconds
- Highlight implications and next steps

### 5. Checklist for Reporting
- [ ] Slide has a single, clear message
- [ ] Visuals support the narrative
- [ ] Speaker notes are concise and actionable

### 6. Example Slide Outline (Markdown)
```
# Journals Drives Retention, Not Total Engagement
- +2.5% Day-28 Retention (Treatment vs Control)
- No significant lift in total time spent
- Engagement loop validated for 30% of users

Speaker Notes:
"This slide summarizes our key finding: Journals is a retention driver, not yet an engagement expander. The feature creates a new habit loop for a subset of users, but overall time in app is unchanged. Next, we should focus on expanding the loop to more users and exploring new engagement levers."
```
